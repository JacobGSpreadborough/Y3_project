\section{Introduction}

Since their inception, mobile phones have become more and more ubiquitous, becoming a tool that is required for everyday life \cite{global-cellphones}.

Mobile devices are involved in almost every aspect of every day life, and have evolved to handle a huge range of tasks. Among these, audio is one of the most important, as it is required for the communication and entertainment capabilities of mobile devices. 

Mobile audio development is constrained mainly by two factors: the speed and quality required for audio processing, and the size and power constraints of a small device with limited battery life. These contstraints are made ever more challenging when attempting to build a single application for multiple mobile phone types, as the methods of controlling audio often involve low level concepts to meet the requirements of the aforementioned factors.

When discussing cross-platform development, the platforms being developed for must be established. In mobile devices, the primary options are Android and iOS \cite{Chandrashekar2021}, with others such as Samsung and Windows falling behind \cite{OS-market-shares}. In this project, the focus will be on development for Android and iOS devices, as together they account for over 90\% of mobile devices in use.

If companies want their apps to be able to reach as many users as possible, then they must develop them for both Android and iOS devices. 
To meet the need to create apps for multiple mobile operating systems, while keeping the cost of development low, then an approach is needed that allows for the development of a single codebase that will create similar apps for the different operating systems.

Despite the ubiquity of mobile devices, and the specific challenges posed by audio development, there is little information in the literature on how a developer should approach this specific problem.
This project serves to provide an outline a comprehensive evaluation and comparision of the tools available for cross-platform audio development, and to provide recommendations for developers based on their specific situation.
